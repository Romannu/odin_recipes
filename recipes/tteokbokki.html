Tteokbokki

Description

Sweet and spicy recipe for one of my favorite south korean dishes.

Very easy to make at home, and it doesn't take a lot of time to do.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg | Tteokbokki
  • 2 | Eggs (2 p/person)
  • 2 | Fish balls (2 p/person)
  • Sauce
    • 3 tbsp | Gochujang
    • 3 tbsp | Sugar
    • 2 tbsp | Soy sauce
    • 3 tbsp | Agave
    • 4 tbsp | Ketchup
    • 2 tbsp | Honey
    • 50 g | Minced garlic

Steps

  1. Boil the fish balls around 10 minutes, or when they begin to float. Don't forget two fish balls per person being served;
  2. Boil the eggs around 10 minutes. Don't forget two eggs per person being served;
  3. while the fish balls and the eggs are boiling, prepare the sauce:
    1. Get a bowl;
    2. Put inside the bowl all the sauce ingredients, in no particular order;
    3. Mix everything with a spoon until it looks uniform;
    4. in this part, you can add portions as you see fit.
  4. Get a big pan and add the sauce you just made, and start boiling it;
  5. When you see the sauce bubbling, you can add a little bit of water (2 cups) and wait for it to bubble again;
  6. Insert the Tteokbokki into the pan;
  7. Add water as you see fit, whilst mixing everything, making sure you cover all the tteokbokki with the sauce;
  8. Boil the tteokbokki until it's very soft (like a pillow);
  9. Add the fish balls and the eggs to the pan and mix everything again, also add water as you see fit;
  10. Boil until the sauce is to your liking, mine is about when its consistency is similar to a yogurt;
  11. And that's it, you did it! When serving I like to put grated cheese on top, but you don't need to. Enjoy!
